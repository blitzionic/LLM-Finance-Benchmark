Question:

Which one of the following statements concerning disbursement float is correct?

A. Disbursement float is the period of time between a firm making a bank deposit and the funds from that deposit being available to the firm.

B. Disbursement float decreases when a check is delayed in the mail due to an extended holiday weekend.

C. Disbursement float causes the available balance to exceed the ledger balance.

D. Disbursement float is being totally eliminated by the Check Clearing Act for the 21st Century.

E. Disbursement float exists when the available balance is less than the book balance.

Disbursement Float:

Financial departments of organizations maintain two types of balances; the book balance, which reflects the true amount the organization is supposed to hold at that time depending on the most recent transactions, and the available balance, which indicates the bank account balance of the company.

Answer and Explanation: 1

  • The correct answer is: C. Disbursement float causes the available balance to exceed the ledger balance.

When a company writes a check, it affects the book balance alone until the check is deposited and cleared by whoever it was intended for. During the period when a check is written but not cleared, the book balance reduces, causing the available balance to exceed it. This is a direct effect of the disbursement float.

Reasons for incorrect options:

The other options provided in this question are simply not correct reflections of the characteristics of disbursement float.
