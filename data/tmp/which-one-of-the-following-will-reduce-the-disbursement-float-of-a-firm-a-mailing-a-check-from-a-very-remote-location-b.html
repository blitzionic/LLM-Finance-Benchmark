Question:

Which one of the following will reduce the disbursement float of a firm?

A. Mailing a check from a very remote location

B. Mailing an unsigned check so that it must be returned for a signature

C. Paying a loan payment at the bank rather than mailing a check to the bank

D. Requiring that all checks be held one day before mailing so they can be reviewed by a manager

E. Writing checks on a zero-balance account rather than on the master account

Disbursement float:

The amount of money that is spent by an individual or organization, but is not deducted from the bank account is known as disbursement float. For example, an individual makes a payment through check, and the bank takes a few days to transfer the amount.

Answer and Explanation: 1

The correct option is C. Paying a loan payment at the bank rather than mailing a check to the bank

When the payment of loan is done in the bank, the disbursement float will decrease as compared to mailing the check. Because the amount has already been spent and when the check is mailed, within 1-2 days the bank will receive the check and further, bank will take some time to proceed the payment.
