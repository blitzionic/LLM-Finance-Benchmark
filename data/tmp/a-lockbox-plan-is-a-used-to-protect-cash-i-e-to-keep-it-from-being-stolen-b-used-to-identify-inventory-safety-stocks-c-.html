Business Finance Cash management

A lockbox plan is: a. used to protect cash, i.e., to keep it from being stolen. b. used to...

Question:

A lockbox plan is:

a. used to protect cash, i.e., to keep it from being stolen.

b. used to identify inventory safety stocks.

c. used to slow down the collection of checks the firm writes.

d. used to speed up the collection of checks received.

e. used primarily by firms where currency is used frequently in transactions, such as fast food restaurants, and less frequently by firms that receive payments as checks.

Check:

A check is a written, dated, and signed document instructing a bank to pay a specific sum of money from the payer's account to the payee. Checks offer a safe and convenient way of transferring money as compared to cash transactions.

Answer and Explanation:

The correct answer is d. used to speed up the collection of checks received.

A lockbox plan is a financial service provided by a bank to streamline the collection of payments, particularly checks, on behalf of a business or organization in exchange for a fee.

The business directs its customers to send payments, usually checks, to a designated post office box or address managed by the bank. This address is often referred to as the "lockbox address."

The bank collects the payments from the lockbox on behalf of the business and processes them by depositing the checks into the business's bank account. The bank then reports the details of the checks deposited to the business.

The business gains access to the funds earlier through the lockbox plan as compared to payments received directly by the business. This accelerated availability of funds improves cash flow and working capital management for the business. So, a lockbox plan is primarily used to speed up the collection of checks received.

  • Lockboxes can offer some level of security by keeping checks in a secure location but these are not designed to protect cash. Hence, answer option a. is incorrect.
  • Lockboxes are used for financial transactions, not inventory management. So, answer option b. is incorrect.
  • Lockboxes aim to expedite the collection of incoming payments. Hence, answer option c. is incorrect.
  • Some businesses in cash-heavy industries might use lockboxes but it is not limited to them. Lockboxes benefit any firm that receives a significant volume of checks. So, answer option e. is incorrect.
