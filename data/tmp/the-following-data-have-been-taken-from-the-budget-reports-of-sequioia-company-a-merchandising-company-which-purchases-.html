Question:

The following data have been taken from the budget reports of Sequioia Company, a merchandising company, which purchases merchandise inventory and sells to customers.

The information on the sales to customers and the purchase of inventory is given below:

Month Sales Purchases
January 100,000 160,000
February 200,000 160,000
March 240,000 160,000
April 280,000 154,000
May 260,000 140,000
Additional Information :

The % of purchases paid for cash at the time of purchase : 40%.

The % of purchases paid in the next month after purchase : 30%.

The % of purchases paid in the second month after purchase : 30%.

Purchases for November and December in the previous year : $150,000 per month.

The Cost of Goods Sold is 60% of sales.

Collections are expected as follows :

60% in month of sale, 40% in month following the sale.

Employee wages are 10% of sales for the month in which the sales occur and are paid during the month.

Other administrative expenses are 20% of the following month's sales and the entire amount is paid during the current month.

The amount includes depreciation expense for the office equipment of $13,000.

Interest payments of $2,100 are paid quarterly in January and April (monthly interest is $700).

Sequoia is planning to declare cash dividends in April to be paid in May : $10,000.

The balance in retained earnings is expected to be $210,000 on March 31.

Required:

Cash disbursements for the month of April would be:

a) 215,200

b) 75,200

c) 157,600

d) 226,700

e) None of the above.

Cash Disbursements

Cash disbursements refer to actual cash payment made by the company at a given period of time. Cash payments may be in the form of bills, coins, checks and electronic bank transfers. Cash disbursements may be different from the expenses reflected in the company's profit and loss statement especially if the company uses an accrual basis of accounting.

Answer and Explanation: 1

The answer is D) $226,700

Solution (Amounts in $):

DisbursementsAprilRemarks
Purchases:
Month of April 61,600 40% of purchases paid for cash at the time of purchase
Month of March 48,000 30% of purchases paid in the next month after purchase.
Month of February 48,000 30% of purchases paid in the second month after purchase
Other expenses:
Employee wages 28,000 10% of sales made for the month which is $280,000
Other admin expenses 39,000 Other administrative expenses are 20% of sales made for the month of May ($260,000) less the depreciation expense of $13,000. Depreciation expense is not being paid in cash and should be excluded in the cash disbursements.
Interest payment 2,100 Interest payments of $2,100 are paid quarterly in January and April.
Total cash disbursements 226,700
