Question:

Explain how each of the following factors would probably affect a firm's target cash balance if all other factors were held constant.

a. The firm institutes a new billing procedure that better synchronizes its cash inflows and outflows.

b. The firm develops a new sales forecasting technique that improves its forecasts.

c. The firm reduces its portfolio of U.S. Treasury bills.

d. The firm arranges to use an overdraft system for its checking account.

e. The firm borrows a large amount of money from its bank and also begins to write far more checks than it did in the past.

f. Interest rates on Treasury bills rise from 5% to 10%.

Cash Management:

Cash management involves making decisions regarding the use of cash in the enterprise: making investments, investing or borrowing.

Answer and Explanation: 1

a) The new procedure will make cash flow management more fluid. This will allow for more precise financial operations that can reduce the business risk (and therefore reduce costs) or increase the company's profitability (and therefore increase revenues).

b) The development of new forecasting techniques will allow more precise determination of production levels and at the same time better adjust production costs and reduce the risk of losses due to overproduction. It will also allow for better marketing activities and maximization of the use of market opportunities - thus obtaining the largest possible revenues.

c) The release of financial means allows to make investments, which will increase the production capacity, thus increasing financial inflows. The operating activity of the company is more profitable than the rate of return from Treasury Bills. Another option for allocating the released funds is to repay the debt with the largest cost, and thus to reduce costs.

d) A debit in a bank account will allow you to increase your financial liquidity, but at the same time increase the operating costs. It is a capital with a relatively high interest rate.

e) Increasing foreign capital in the company will increase costs. However, if the money is spent on investment in operating activities, it may also lead to an increase in revenues. The final balance of cash will depend on the ratio of the interest rate on debt and the return on investment.

f) A two-fold increase in the Treasury Bills interest rate can work in two ways. If the company invests money in this way, the interest income will increase. An increase in the Treasury Bills interest rate may, however, translate into a general increase in the cost of capital - that is why the cost of foreign capital may increase.
